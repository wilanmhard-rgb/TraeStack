# Design Consultation

Turn fuzzy product taste into a coherent design system the team can actually build against.

## When To Use
- the user wants help defining visual direction before building UI
- a repo needs a DESIGN.md, refreshed brand system, or product-specific design rules
- the team wants research-backed typography, color, spacing, layout, and motion guidance
- a plan or product feels visually generic and needs a more opinionated design thesis

## Core Rules
- propose a complete system, not a disconnected menu of style options
- ground recommendations in product type, audience, and the one thing the product should be remembered for
- reuse existing product context, DESIGN.md, and prior taste signals before inventing new direction
- avoid generic AI-design tropes such as default gradients, template card grids, and safe-but-forgettable font choices
- keep the result implementable by naming real tokens, patterns, and constraints
- write or update durable design guidance when the user wants the system captured for future work

## Workflow
1. Gather product context from the repo, prior artifacts, and the user's stated goals.
2. Confirm audience, product type, desired feeling, and the most memorable trait the design should communicate.
3. Optionally research the category, then synthesize where to follow conventions and where to take deliberate visual risks.
4. Propose one coherent design system covering aesthetic direction, typography, color, spacing, layout, and motion.
5. Refine based on feedback, then produce the durable artifact the project needs, usually `DESIGN.md` plus preview or mockup guidance.

## Output
- concise product and design brief
- opinionated design-system proposal with concrete tokens and rationale
- safe choices versus differentiating risks
- recommended next step such as `design-shotgun` for exploration or `design-html` for implementation
- `DESIGN.md` plan or update when the user wants the system written down
